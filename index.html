About / artist statement

Statement

I am drawn to creation as a way of studying relation. These photographs begin with landscape, atmosphere, and the built environment, but they are less about documenting scene than about tracing encounter—how bodies, weather, structure, memory, and more-than-human life participate in shaping what becomes visible.

Much of this work is informed by my broader research interests in relationality, intra-action, ecological entanglement, mental health, and the role of imagination in sensing and creating more liveable futures. I am interested in how art can help us feel our relation within the world rather than reinforce the myth of being separate from it. In this sense, photography becomes not only an aesthetic practice, but also a way of noticing how place acts on us, how we act within it, and how life itself is co-constituted.
